A Collection of Clever Integral Tricks

Jul 11 2019 Mathematics 14 minutes read (About 2037 words)

This post is my collection of obscure, rare, or asthetically pleasing integral tricks. Some of them use clever substitutions, others exploit the symmetry of the integrand, or make use of some devilishly sly maneuvers such as differentiating under the integral sign. For a quick appetizer, try evaluate
$$
\int_0^\pi\ln(a^2-2a\cos x+1)\,\mathrm d x
$$where \(a>0\). This is one of my favorite integrals. We will see later in this post that this integral can be solved by at least five different methods, which is simply astounding! I should clearify that not all of these tricks are meant to be pragmatic, and the vast majority of tricks in this collection are for recreational mathematics. The integral tricks in this collection mainly comes from past papers of math olympiads such as the Putnam, PUMaC, HMMT and SMT, as well as online math communities like Math Stack Exchange and Art of Problem Solving. For further reading, I really recommend the book Inside Interesting Integrals by Paul J. Nahin, who does a better job of summarizing integral tricks than I do.
